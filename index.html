Mathematics: Understanding Areas

Area is the amount of space inside a two-dimensional shape. It's measured in square units and is fundamental in mathematics, architecture, engineering, and many daily life applications.

Square

A square is a quadrilateral with all four sides equal and all angles right angles (90°).

Formula: Area = side × side = s²

Since all sides are equal, finding the area is straightforward - just multiply the length of one side by itself.

Example: If a square has a side length of 5 cm, its area is 5 × 5 = 25 cm².

Area: 10000 sq units

Rectangle

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with opposite sides equal and all angles right angles (90°).

Formula: Area = length × width = l × w

To find the area of a rectangle, multiply its length by its width.

Example: If a rectangle has a length of 8 cm and width of 5 cm, its area is 8 × 5 = 40 cm².

Area: 9600 sq units

Triangle

A triangle is a polygon with three edges and three vertices. It's the simplest polygon.

Formula: Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × b × h

The height (altitude) of a triangle is the perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex.

Example: If a triangle has a base of 6 cm and height of 4 cm, its area is ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm².

Area: 4000 sq units

Circle

A circle is a shape consisting of all points in a plane that are a given distance from a center point.

Formula: Area = π × radius² = πr²

The radius (r) is the distance from the center of the circle to any point on its edge. π (pi) is approximately 3.14159.

Example: If a circle has a radius of 3 cm, its area is π × 3² ≈ 3.1416 × 9 ≈ 28.27 cm².

Area: 20106 sq units

Regular Polygon

A regular polygon is a polygon that is both equiangular (all angles equal) and equilateral (all sides equal).

Formula: Area = ½ × perimeter × apothem

The apothem is the distance from the center to the midpoint of any side. Regular polygons can be divided into congruent isosceles triangles.

Example: A regular hexagon with side length 4 cm and apothem 3.46 cm has an area of ½ × 24 × 3.46 ≈ 41.52 cm².

Area: 9511 sq units

Summary of Area Formulas

Square

A = s²

s = side length

Rectangle

A = l × w

l = length, w = width

Triangle

A = ½ × b × h

b = base, h = height

Circle

A = πr²

r = radius, π ≈ 3.1416

Regular Polygon

A = ½ × p × a

p = perimeter, a = apothem
